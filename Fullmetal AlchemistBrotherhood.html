Fullmetal Alchemist: Brotherhood

鋼の錬金術師
7.85 Votes

After a horrific alchemy experiment goes wrong in the Elric household, brothers Edward and Alphonse are left in a catastrophic new reality. Ignoring the alchemical principle banning human transmutation, the boys attempted to bring their recently deceased mother back to life. Instead, they suffered brutal personal loss: Alphonse's body disintegrated while Edward lost a leg and then sacrificed an arm to keep Alphonse's soul in the physical realm by binding it to a hulking suit of armor. The brothers are rescued by their neighbor Pinako Rockbell and her granddaughter Winry. Known as a bio-mechanical engineering prodigy, Winry creates prosthetic limbs for Edward by utilizing "automail," a tough, versatile metal used in robots and combat armor. After years of training, the Elric brothers set off on a quest to restore their bodies by locating the Philosopher's Stone—a powerful gem that allows an alchemist to defy the traditional laws of Equivalent Exchange. As Edward becomes an infamous alchemist and gains the nickname "Fullmetal," the boys' journey embroils them in a growing conspiracy that threatens the fate of the world.

Jiya Shah - 1 Hour ago

This is a very good show, with even better characters, some of the most well written I’ve seen. The action is good, and it’s not overdone, which I can appreciate. However I feel like the action is done tastefully, and it’s not every episode so when it does happen it is almost like a payoff to the last few episodes. I’ll admit I had my doubts about this series at first. What was so good about it? The story seemed average at first, and I was thinking alchemy was basically Naruto hand signs. (still, I tried to be open minded when watching it). But there’s something about this show that makes me want to stick with it. The characters are definitely a major reason, as the Seven Deadly Sins are amazing, especially Envy and Greed. Scar and Kimblee are cool, and Hoenheim steals the show whenever he’s onscreen. The characters like Scar, Ling, Edward and Alphonse all have their own motives, but somehow the story manages to bring all the characters’ arcs together nicely(quite literally with Greedling), while centering around the Philospher’s Stone. Bradley’s reveal as Wrath was very interesting, because there was something about the way it was executed that made it so satisfying to watch. The same goes for Roy Mustang and Riza Hawkeye. Other than the characters, this show also has great humor .
